Chole Bhature

A quintessential North Indian breakfast recipe, relished by one and all can now be easily cooked at home. Straight from the Punjabi kitchen - hot and delicious, chole bhature! Learn the art of making feather soft bhaturas served with chole or chickpeas cooked in a pool of rustic spices.

prep steps

16 steps.

cook time

1hr 15mins

serving

10 servings

Tags : South North Italian Chinise Drinks desserts

instructions

step 1

In a pan add oil, bay leaf, cinnamon, cumin seeds, cloves, whole pepper corns, green and black cardamom.

step 2

After it gets brown add chopped onions and saute it. Now add chopped ginger and garlic.

step 3

Followed by turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, asafoetida and salt, fry the ingredients together well. For de--glazing the pan add a little water.

step 4

Now add the chole (soaked overnight and pressure cooked) to the masala.

step 5

After stirring well add tomatoes, little sugar and salt to the chole.

step 6

Now add ajwain, chopped green chilies and water for the base. To get the color in the chole, add a tea bag to the masala.

step 7

Simmer the chole gently for an hour and cover it. Add lime juice and a dollop of butter to it.

step 8

Garnish the chole with coriander and butter and serve them hot with bhaturas.

Prepare the bhaturas:

step 1

Knead the whole wheat flour, maida and salt together with adequate amount of water.

step 2

Sprinkle the yeast on top. Leave it for 2-3 hours for the yeast to work

step 3

Divide it into equal portions. Roll out in an oval or round shape. Deep fry till golden brown, like a puri and server hot.

ingredients

chickpeas (channas) 2 cups

oil 2 teaspoon

Bay leaf (tej patta) 1

Cloves 3-4

whole pepper corns 1 teaspoon

Green cardamom 3

turmeric powder 1 tsp

chili powder 1 tsp

whole wheat flour 1/2 cup

Water as required

tomatoes 1 cup

ginger 1 tsp

ajwain 1 tsp

asafoetida 1/2 tsp

butter 1 tbsp
