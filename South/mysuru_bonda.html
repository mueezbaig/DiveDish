Mysore Bonda

This Mysore Bonda recipe (also known as Mysore Bajji) is a popular South Indian snack food made with lentils, chopped fresh coconut, fresh herbs and vibrant spices. The ingredients are combined to create a thick batter, and are then deep fried to make crisp, soft and fluffy fritters.

prep steps

14 steps.

cook time

30 min.

serving

12 servings

Tags : South North Italian Chinise Drinks desserts

instructions

step 1

Firstly, in a large bowl take 1 cup curd, Â¾ tsp salt and 1 tsp baking soda.

step 2

Mix well until the curd turns frothy.

step 3

Now add 2 cup maida and start to mix gently.

step 4

Adding more curd if required, start to beat in 1 direction.

step 5

The batter will turn elastic texture.

step 6

Now add 2 chilli, 1 inch ginger, few curry leaves, 2 tbsp coconut, 2 tbsp coriander and 1 tsp cumin.

step 7

Mix well combining everything well. Now, cover and rest for 4 hours.

step 8

After 4 hours, the batter will turn more soft and fluffy.

step 9

Dip your hand in the water and pinch a ball sized batter. Drop in hot oil, keeping the flame on medium.

step 10

Stir and fry until the bonda turns golden brown and crisp.

step 11

Finally, drain off the mysore bajji and enjoy with chutney

ingredients

Baking soda 1 tbsp

Maida 2 cup

Curd 1 cup

Salt 2 tsp

Dry coconut 1/4 cup

coriander 2 tbsp

Curry leaves 10 -15

cumin 1 tbsp
